Game Rules

Setup

Place the game board in the center of the play-space, with the pile of Blood Tokens reachable for all players. Shuffle the location tiles with their symbols up, and place each at a location with the matching symbol, face down.

Each player chooses a faction to play as containing Units, a Faction Sheet, an Action Deck, and a Victory Deck. Check the Factions Page for unique setups for certain factions.

Cards

There are five types of cards in Bloodletting:

  • Victory Cards – Used to defeat units in contested locations.
  • Plots – Played into the combat pile when contested, if readied during Planning.
  • Schemes – Played into the combat pile once per combat phase by any player.
  • Instantaneous Cards – Played during the Planning Phase directly.
  • Active Cards – Provide ongoing effects as long as they are readied.

The Board

The board is a series of open locations connected by paths. Players may move units between them to gain advantages.

  • Villages: Gain 1 blood per Village occupied.
  • Ruins: Increase hand size by 2 per Ruin occupied.
  • Farmland: Units moving from Farmland get +1 speed.

How to Play

Game Start

Players collect 2 Blood-Tokens and draw 4 cards. Choose a first player and give them the Initiative token.

Planning Phase

Players may perform these actions in any order:

  • Summon units
  • Move units
  • Ready action cards
  • Resolve Instantaneous cards
  • Discard cards

End the turn by declaring. Continue clockwise until it returns to the Initiative holder.

Combat Phase

  1. Add Victory cards equal to contested strength.
  2. Add readied Plots for contested units.
  3. Add one readied Scheme per player.
  4. Shuffle and resolve combat pile.

End of Combat Phase:

  • Return Victory cards to piles
  • Discard resolved actions
  • Pass Initiative clockwise
  • Gain 2 Blood (+1 per Village)
  • Draw cards to hand size

Repeat phases until one faction meets their win condition.
